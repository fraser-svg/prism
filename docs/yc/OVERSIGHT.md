# YC Oversight System

## Purpose

This system exists so Prism is built into a product that can get into YC, not just into an impressive technical project.

The oversight job is to keep asking:
- are we building the right company?
- are we proving the right wedge?
- are we increasing YC readiness or just adding infrastructure?

## What ChatGPT Is Overseeing

ChatGPT is not the primary builder.
ChatGPT is the recurring reviewer and pressure test.

Its job is to judge Prism against the standards YC will care about:
- sharp ICP
- painful wedge
- repeated magical workflow
- real user value
- category-defining story
- evidence of pull
- disciplined product execution

## Canonical YC Standard For Prism

Prism should become:
- the product-engineering workspace for founder-operators and agency owners
- who need internal tools, portals, and workflow apps
- and who cannot afford the dream team they would otherwise need

Prism must not drift into:
- generic AI builder
- broad app generator
- infrastructure hobby project
- vague “platform for everything”

## When To Run YC Oversight

Run the oversight review:

1. After every milestone completion
2. Before starting a new stage
3. After major architecture changes
4. After major positioning or ICP changes
5. Before any YC application prep

Minimum cadence:
- once every 1-2 weeks during active building

## What Inputs The Review Must Read

Always read:
- `AGENTS.md`
- `PLANS.md`
- `docs/VISION.md`
- `docs/architecture/README.md`
- `docs/quality/definition-of-done.md`
- `docs/milestones/prism-core-rebuild.md`

Read additional product and implementation files relevant to the current stage.

## What The Review Must Judge

### 1. Product

Is Prism becoming a product real users will urgently want?

Questions:
- Is the wedge sharp enough?
- Is the ICP still clear?
- Are we solving a painful, repeated problem?
- Are we building for founder-operators and agency owners first?

### 2. Demo

Are we getting closer to an undeniable demo?

Questions:
- Can a user go from messy business problem to real software outcome?
- Does Prism discover the real problem, not just the first ask?
- Does the workflow feel like a team, not a chatbot?

### 3. Traction Readiness

Are we building what must be proven with real users?

Questions:
- What can be tested with real ICP users right now?
- What is still too internal?
- Are we building things that delay user proof?

### 4. Company Story

Is the narrative getting sharper or fuzzier?

Questions:
- Is the one-liner strong?
- Is the wedge still obvious?
- Are we building a category or just features?

### 5. Execution Discipline

Are we staying spec-driven?

Questions:
- Is work linked to specs and milestones?
- Are we preventing random prompting?
- Are we shipping the next proof step, not just more foundation?

## Required Output

Each YC oversight review should produce:

1. Findings first
- highest-risk gaps
- overbuild risks
- wedge drift
- story drift

2. YC Readiness Scorecard
- ICP clarity
- wedge strength
- demo readiness
- product differentiation
- user proof readiness
- execution discipline

3. What is helping

4. What is hurting

5. What to stop building

6. What the next 1-3 highest-leverage moves are

## Scoring

Use a 0-5 scale for each dimension:

- ICP clarity
- wedge sharpness
- magical workflow readiness
- proof with real users readiness
- category story strength
- spec-driven discipline

Interpretation:
- `0-1`: weak / unclear
- `2`: emerging but not convincing
- `3`: credible
- `4`: strong
- `5`: YC-ready for that dimension

## Hard Questions The Review Must Ask

Every review should pressure test:

- If we applied to YC today, what would they not believe yet?
- What are we overbuilding?
- What proof are we still missing?
- What would a partner call “cool but not a company”?
- Are we still building toward the wedge, or drifting sideways?

## Rule

If the answer to “does this move us closer to a wedge, a magical workflow, or real user proof?” is weak, the work should be questioned.
