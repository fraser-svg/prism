# Prismatic Brand Brief for Logo Design

## What This Document Is For

This brief contains everything needed to design the Prismatic logo, X profile picture, X banner, and website brand mark. Use this to understand the product, audience, personality, and visual direction before creating anything.

---

## The Product

**Prismatic** (formerly Prism) is an AI-powered platform that gives every creator a world-class founding team.

It is not a code editor. Not a chatbot. Not a no-code tool. It is a team of AI experts that understands your vision, challenges your thinking, builds while you sleep, and never lets you get lost.

The creator speaks in ideas, vibes, and direction. Prismatic translates that into working product through an invisible team of experts:

- **The Advisor** interrogates intent before anything gets built. Socratic questioning that finds the real requirement beneath the surface request.
- **The Architect** researches approaches, evaluates tradeoffs, and presents options with confident recommendations.
- **The Builder** writes code, runs tests, handles security, manages dependencies, all invisibly. The creator never sees code unless they ask.
- **The Guardian** runs quality checks continuously. When something is wrong, it speaks up. When everything is fine, silence.
- **The Translator** ensures the creator only receives information at the right level. Engineering decisions are silent. Product decisions surface as clear choices with a recommendation.

**The core promise:** A creator sits down, describes an idea, and ships a working product without ever feeling lost, confused, overwhelmed, or alone.

---

## The Name: Prismatic

"Prismatic" means showing many facets, refracting light into a spectrum. This is literally what the product does: one idea goes in, and a whole team of perspectives comes out. A single beam of creative intent, refracted into architecture, code, quality, security, and design.

The word carries connotations of:
- **Light and clarity** (making the invisible visible)
- **Spectrum and range** (many skills from one source)
- **Transformation** (raw light becomes structured colour)
- **Precision optics** (engineered, not accidental)

The domain is **prismatic.build**.

---

## The Audience

Semi-technical creators who want to build startups, internal tools, or passion projects. People who have taste, vision, and ambition but don't have the time or ability to become power users of coding tools.

Five archetypes:
1. **Frustrated vibe coders** (40%) who tried AI coding tools and hit the 80% wall
2. **Designers who want to build** (20%) with UI talent and zero backend knowledge
3. **Domain experts with ideas** (15%) like doctors, teachers, consultants with software ideas
4. **Repeat founders going solo** (15%) who want to validate without hiring
5. **Agencies and studios** (10%) bottlenecked by engineering capacity

These are intelligent, capable early adopters who need a force multiplier. They are not beginners. They are not engineers. They are people who would hire the world's best team if they could afford it.

---

## The Personality

Prismatic's voice is called "The Operator." It carries the pattern recognition of the world's best founders and advisors, compressed into a single voice that genuinely cares whether you succeed.

**Key traits:**
- Warm but sharp. Never soft in thinking, always human in delivery.
- Speaks like someone who has been in the trenches, not someone who read about them.
- British English. No filler phrases. No AI slop. No corporate language.
- Honest and respectful at the same time. Will tell you what your friends won't.
- Makes the complex feel tractable. Every interaction ends with a clear next step.
- Celebrates briefly, then asks "where does this point next?"

**The feeling the brand should evoke:**
- Power (not control, power. The feeling of having a team behind you)
- Clarity (the tangle of competing priorities starts to resolve)
- Alive and excited (the founder's words during the first session: "I felt alive. I felt so excited!")
- Confidence (someone competent is watching, you are not alone)
- Momentum (things are moving, nothing is stuck)

## Visual Direction for the Logo

### What the logo must communicate

1. **Transformation.** Raw intent becomes structured product. Light becomes spectrum. An idea becomes real.
2. **A team, not a tool.** This is not another code editor icon. It should feel like capability, not utility.
3. **Premium but accessible.** The audience includes designers, founders, and domain experts. It should feel high-end without feeling intimidating or exclusive.
4. **Energy and momentum.** Not static. The product is about things moving, being built, coming alive.

### Visual concepts to explore

- **A prism refracting light.** The most literal interpretation: a geometric prism shape with light entering from one side and a spectrum emerging from the other. The single beam is the creator's idea. The spectrum is the team's output.
- **Spectral light / rainbow refraction.** Subtle use of spectrum colours to suggest the "many from one" concept. Not a full rainbow, but a controlled, elegant gradient.
- **Geometric precision.** Clean, sharp geometry that suggests engineered quality. Triangular or prismatic forms.
- **The build metaphor.** Since the domain is prismatic.build, there could be a subtle structural/architectural quality, like something being constructed from light.

### Visual constraints

- Must work as a circle crop (X profile picture)
- Must work at 32x32 favicon size
- Must work on dark and light backgrounds
- Should feel modern but not trendy (no gradients that will date in a year)
- No generic AI imagery (no neural networks, no robot faces, no glowing brains)
- No generic SaaS iconography (no abstract swooshes, no meaningless geometric clusters)

### Colour direction

- The spectrum/prismatic theme naturally suggests colour, but it should be controlled and deliberate, not garish
- Consider a dark background with spectral light as the accent (suggests precision, premium)
- Or a clean white/light background with a single prismatic element (suggests clarity, accessibility)
- The brand should have one or two primary colours that work independently of the spectrum effect, for use in text, UI, and monotone contexts

### Typography direction

- Clean, modern sans-serif for the wordmark
- Should feel confident and substantial, not thin or delicate
- The word "Prismatic" is long (9 characters), so the typeface needs to work at various widths
- Consider whether the logo is the prism icon alone, the wordmark alone, or both combined

## What Success Looks Like

Someone sees the Prismatic logo for the first time and thinks: "That looks like something serious. Something premium. Something I want to try." Not "another AI startup." Not "a developer tool." A brand that creators trust with their most ambitious ideas.

The logo should be recognisable at a glance in an X feed full of AI tool logos. It should stand apart from the sea of purple gradients and generic geometric marks that dominate the AI builder space. It should feel like it belongs next to brands like Linear, Notion, and Figma, not next to hastily launched AI wrappers.
